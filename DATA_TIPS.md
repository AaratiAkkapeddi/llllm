# Building Your Dataset

## How Many PDFs?

**some guidelines:**

- Your PDFs should each be only a maximum of 30 pages (this is to avoid long build times). So if you have one that's really long, you should split it up into multiple pdfs,
- In total across all your pdfs, you want to have a minimum of 50–100 pages (to get decent results) and no more than 300–500 pages (to avoid slow process time)
- But besides that range, it really depends on the context of the content and the relationship between your pdfs. So some trial and error is definitely involved.

---

## What Kinds of Documents Work Best?

Typically people use journal articles, academic papers, essays, these kinds of things. But this class is about experimenting with your own handmade dataset so I encourage you to experiment. 

### But some technical caveats:

**Look out for (still works, but not the best):**
- Scanned PDFs — text may not extract cleanly if the scan quality is low
- PDFs with lots of images, charts, or tables — the AI reads text only, visuals are ignored
- Slides/presentations — often too fragmented and bullet-point heavy to chunk well
- PDFs with two-column layouts — text extraction can scramble the reading order

**You need to avoid:**
- Password-protected PDFs
- PDFs that are just images with no selectable text (scanned without OCR)
- Highly formatted documents where the meaning depends on layout (e.g. spreadsheets saved as PDF)

**In terms of content:**
- documents that are related to each other work better than random unrelated files. 
- at the same time, you might want to think about diversity of your dataset. For example, for practical usecases it could be helpful to have more than one author/perspective represented in your corpus.
- think about what questions you want to ask, then make sure the answers are actually in your documents.

----

## Tips for Organizing Your Docs Folder

- Use descriptive filenames — `foucault_ch3.pdf` is much better than `scan0047.pdf`
- Keep all PDFs in the `./docs/` folder before running `rag.py build`
- If you want to start fresh with a new dataset, delete the database folder that's generated by `rag.py build` (it should be called something like rag_database) and rebuild:
```bash
python3 rag.py build   # Mac
python rag.py build    # Windows/PC
```
